## 1. Google Business Profile

**Cost:** 100% FREE
**How to Sign Up:** Go to business.google.com, create or claim your business listing. Verify via postcard, phone, or email.
**How to Get Leads:** Homeowners searching "handyman near me" or "handyman Jacksonville FL" find your listing in Google Maps and the Local 3-Pack (top 3 results shown on Google Search).

**Optimization for Jacksonville FL:**
- Set your service area to cover Jacksonville, Orange Park, Atlantic Beach, Neptune Beach, Arlington, Mandarin, Riverside, San Marco, Southside, Baymeadows, and surrounding Duval County areas
- Select primary category "Handyman" and add secondary categories: "Home Improvement Service," "General Contractor," "Home Repair Service"
- Upload at least 10 high-quality before/after photos of completed work (geotag them in Jacksonville)
- Write a 750-word business description loaded with local keywords like "Jacksonville handyman," "Duval County home repair," "Northeast Florida maintenance"
- Add attributes: "Veteran-owned" or "Locally owned" if applicable
- Post weekly Google Updates (photos, offers, tips)
- Actively request reviews from every customer -- aim for 25+ reviews within first 3 months
- Respond to every review (positive and negative) within 24 hours

**Realistic Leads per Month:** 5-20 leads/month once optimized with 20+ reviews. Can grow to 30-50/month once you rank in the Local 3-Pack.
**Quality Rating:** 9/10 -- Highest intent leads (people actively searching for your service)
**The Catch:** Takes 2-4 months to build enough reviews and authority to rank well. Requires consistent posting and review management.
**Verdict: ABSOLUTELY DO THIS FIRST. Non-negotiable.**

## 2. Nextdoor

**Cost:** FREE business page. Paid Local Deals start around $3-75 depending on neighborhood size and duration.
**How to Sign Up:** Go to business.nextdoor.com, claim or create your free Business Page. Must verify a real business address.
**How to Get Leads:**
- Neighbors post requests like "Anyone know a good handyman?" -- you respond
- You get 2 free Business Posts per month in the neighborhood newsfeed
- Neighbors recommend you in posts, creating word-of-mouth snowball
- Respond to all recommendation requests in your service area

**Posting Strategy:**
- Post helpful tips (seasonal maintenance checklists, DIY advice) -- builds trust
- Share before/after photos of local jobs (with client permission)
- Offer a "Nextdoor Neighbor Discount" to encourage bookings
- Engage in community discussions (be helpful, not salesy)
- Ask happy clients to recommend you on Nextdoor specifically
- Join as a personal member in your neighborhood too -- engage in conversations

**Realistic Leads per Month:** 3-10 leads/month in active neighborhoods. Can be higher during spring/summer home improvement season.
**Quality Rating:** 8/10 -- High trust factor because it is a neighborhood-based platform
**The Catch:** Some neighborhoods are more active than others. You can only post organically 2x/month for free. Growth is slow at first but compounds as recommendations build.
**Verdict: HIGH PRIORITY. The trust factor makes these leads very warm.**

## 3. Yelp

**Cost:** FREE to create and claim a business page. Paid ads start around $150-300/month (NOT required).
**How to Sign Up:** Go to biz.yelp.com/claim, enter your business name, verify ownership.
**How to Get Leads:**
- Homeowners search for "handyman" on Yelp and find your listing
- Yelp's "Request a Quote" feature lets homeowners fill out project details and send directly to you -- completely free
- Businesses with complete profiles receive 5x more leads than minimal profiles
- Profiles with photos receive 36% more customer leads

**Optimization Checklist:**
- Complete 100% of profile fields (5,000-character business description)
- Upload 20+ high-quality photos of completed work
- Add all service categories (handyman, drywall repair, painting, plumbing, etc.)
- Set accurate service area and business hours
- Add attributes: "Offers military discount," "Licensed," "Insured"
- Respond to every review within 24 hours
- Use the "Request a Quote" feature prominently

**Realistic Leads per Month:** 2-8 leads/month with a solid free listing. Can increase to 10-20/month with 30+ positive reviews.
**Quality Rating:** 7/10 -- Good intent, but some leads are price-shopping
**The Catch:** Yelp's algorithm can filter reviews (some legitimate reviews disappear). They will aggressively try to sell you ads. Do NOT buy ads unless you have a solid ROI baseline. Yelp sometimes places competitor ads on your free listing page.
**Verdict: WORTH IT for the free listing. Ignore the sales calls.**

## 4. Porch.com

**Cost:** FREE to create a profile. Lead costs range $5-60+ depending on service type. Optional "Vetted Pro" status costs ~$360/year.
**How to Sign Up:** Go to pro.porch.com, create your business profile, add services and service area.
**How to Get Leads:**
- Porch partners with Lowe's -- homeowners buying supplies at Lowe's get connected to local pros
- Homeowners submit project requests on Porch.com and matched pros receive leads
- You choose to pay per lead or set a monthly budget
- Lead costs for handyman services typically $5-35 per lead

**What Makes Porch Different:**
- Lowe's partnership gives them unique lead source (in-store customers)
- More flexible payment options than competitors
- Lead costs tend to be lower than Thumbtack or Angi for handyman work
- "Vetted Pro" badge adds credibility but is not required

**Realistic Leads per Month:** 3-10 leads/month depending on budget. Free profile alone generates very few leads.
**Quality Rating:** 6/10 -- Mixed quality. Lowe's-sourced leads tend to be better.
**The Catch:** Free profile gets minimal visibility. Real leads require paying per lead. Lead quality is inconsistent -- some are tire-kickers.
**Verdict: CREATE THE FREE PROFILE. Only pay for leads after you have revenue from free platforms.**

## 5. Home Depot Pro Referral

**Cost:** FREE to join. You earn leads by shopping at Home Depot (spending = points = leads).
**How to Sign Up:** Go to proreferral.homedepot.com, create profile (takes ~15 minutes), submit required documents.
**Required Documents:**
- Proof of General Liability insurance
- Contractor license (if required by FL law for your services)
- Personal ID with photo (driver's license or passport)

**How the Points System Works:**
- Money spent at Home Depot earns points in your Pro Referral account
- Points convert into job leads
- Each referral costs 40-80 points depending on service type
- Both in-store and online purchases count toward points
- Home Depot customers looking for a local pro are matched to your profile

**Realistic Leads per Month:** 2-8 leads/month, depending on your spending level. If you already buy supplies at Home Depot, this is essentially free leads.
**Quality Rating:** 7/10 -- These customers are actively at Home Depot buying materials, so they are serious about their projects.
**The Catch:** You must be regularly spending at Home Depot to accumulate points. If you don't buy supplies there, this doesn't help. Also requires insurance and proper documentation.
**Verdict: GREAT if you already shop at Home Depot. Sign up immediately if you have insurance and a license.**

## 6. BuildZoom

**Cost:** FREE to claim profile. 2.5% commission ONLY if you get hired through the platform. Optional $99/month premium listing.
**How to Sign Up:** BuildZoom automatically creates profiles from public contractor license data. Search for your business at buildzoom.com, claim your profile, and enhance it with photos, descriptions, and reviews.

**How It Works:**
- Homeowners post projects on BuildZoom
- BuildZoom matches them with relevant contractors
- You only pay the 2.5% referral fee if the homeowner actually hires you
- Example: $5,000 job = $125 to BuildZoom. $500 job = $12.50 to BuildZoom.

**Profile Optimization:**
- Upload portfolio photos of completed work
- Verify all contact information
- Collect reviews from past clients on the platform
- Complete all profile sections thoroughly

**Realistic Leads per Month:** 1-5 leads/month. Lower volume but zero risk since you only pay if hired.
**Quality Rating:** 7/10 -- Leads are pre-qualified since BuildZoom helps match projects to relevant contractors.
**The Catch:** Lower lead volume than bigger platforms. The 2.5% commission can add up on larger jobs. Lead flow depends on how many homeowners in Jacksonville use the platform.
**Verdict: ABSOLUTELY SIGN UP. Zero upfront cost, zero risk. Only pay when you earn.**

## 7. Houzz

**Cost:** FREE basic profile and portfolio. Premium "Houzz Pro Essential" is a paid upgrade.
**How to Sign Up:** Go to pro.houzz.com, create your profile. Add company info, services, service area.

**How It Generates Leads:**
- Your profile appears in the "Find Professionals" directory when homeowners search by location
- Every project photo you upload links back to your profile
- Homeowners can contact you directly via phone or email from your profile
- Before/after project galleries drive organic traffic

**Optimization:**
- Upload 20+ high-quality project photos with detailed descriptions
- Organize photos into project galleries (kitchen remodel, bathroom repair, etc.)
- Get past clients to write reviews on Houzz
- Fill out every profile section completely
- Respond to homeowner questions in the Houzz Q&A forums (builds visibility)

**Best For:** Remodeling, renovation, and higher-end handyman work. Less effective for small repairs.
**Realistic Leads per Month:** 1-5 leads/month. Better for larger projects ($1,000+).
**Quality Rating:** 7/10 -- Houzz users tend to have higher budgets and are planning serious projects.
**The Catch:** Works best for visual, portfolio-worthy work. Basic handyman tasks (leaky faucets, door fixes) don't generate much traction here. More useful for remodeling/renovation services.
**Verdict: WORTH IT if you do remodeling or renovation work. Create the free profile either way.**

## 8. Thumbtack

**Cost:** FREE to create profile. Pay-per-lead model. Lead costs typically $20-80+ depending on job type and competition.
**How to Sign Up:** Go to thumbtack.com/pro, create profile, add services and service area.

**How It Works:**
- Homeowners submit project requests
- Thumbtack matches them with up to 5 pros
- You choose which leads to respond to (and pay for)
- You can set daily/weekly budget caps

**Free Credits for New Signups:**
- No standard free credit offer for direct signups
- Some integration partners (like Contractor+) offer $100 in free leads upon signup through their platform
- You may receive occasional free leads if no other contractors are available in your area
- Turning on "Thumbtack Promote" (auto-respond to matching leads) gives a 20% discount on credits

**Maximizing Value:**
- Be extremely selective about which leads you respond to
- Respond within minutes -- first responder has highest close rate
- Set a strict weekly budget and track your cost per acquired customer
- Focus on higher-ticket jobs where the lead cost is a smaller percentage
- Target leads during off-peak times when competition is lower

**Realistic Leads per Month:** 5-20+ leads depending on budget. Without paying, essentially zero.
**Quality Rating:** 6/10 -- Mixed. Some leads are serious, many are price-shopping. High competition.
**The Catch:** Can get expensive fast. Lead quality varies wildly. Many homeowners send requests to multiple platforms simultaneously. No guarantee the lead will hire you.
**Verdict: START SMALL. Set a $50/week budget and track ROI carefully. Only increase if profitable.**

## 9. Bark.com

**Cost:** FREE to join and receive leads. Pay credits to respond to leads you want. Credits cost ~$1.57-2.35 each depending on bulk purchase.
**How to Sign Up:** Go to bark.com, create a professional profile, set your services and location.

**How It Works:**
- Homeowners ("Barkers") submit service requests
- Bark sends matching leads to relevant professionals
- You see the lead details for free
- You spend credits to send a quote/response to leads you want
- Credit cost per lead varies by job type and value (2-15+ credits per response)

**Free Credits:**
- No specific free credit offer for new signups currently advertised
- Credits purchased in bulk get discounted rates
- Credits expire 3 months after purchase (as of November 2025 policy)

**Maximizing Value:**
- Only respond to leads that match your strengths and service area exactly
- Personalize every response (generic templates get ignored)
- Respond quickly -- speed matters
- Start with a small credit purchase to test lead quality in your area

**Realistic Leads per Month:** Depends on credits purchased. Budget $50-100/month in credits to test.
**Quality Rating:** 5/10 -- Lower than Thumbtack. Leads are often early-stage explorers.
**The Catch:** Credit expiration after 3 months is harsh. Lead quality is inconsistent. Multiple pros respond to each lead, so competition is stiff. No guarantee of response from the homeowner.
**Verdict: LOW PRIORITY. Test with a small credit purchase after establishing free platform presence.**

**Cost:** FREE basic profile. Paid lead programs: ~$300/year membership + $15-85 per lead.
**How to Sign Up:** Go to angi.com, claim or create your business profile.

**Free Profile Features:**
- Basic business listing with company info

- Profile page visible to homeowners searching Angi

**What Free Gets You:**

- Free listings don't appear at the top of search results
- You can collect reviews, which helps long-term credibility

**Paid Options (For When You Have Budget):**
- Annual membership (~$300) unlocks lead generation
- Per-lead costs: $15-85 depending on trade (handyman is on the lower end)

**Realistic Leads per Month (Free):** 0-3 leads/month. Free profile is essentially a placeholder.
**Realistic Leads per Month (Paid):** 5-20+ depending on budget and service area.
**Quality Rating:** 6/10 -- Reviews from contractors are mixed. Some report good ROI, many report wasted money.
**The Catch:** Free profile generates almost nothing. The paid program requires significant investment. Lead quality has reportedly declined. Aggressive sales team will push hard for paid subscriptions.
**Verdict: CLAIM THE FREE PROFILE for SEO purposes. Do NOT pay for leads until you have steady income from free sources.**

# PART 2: PLATFORMS THAT GIVE FREE CREDITS TO START

## Current Free Credit/Trial Offers (as of March 2026)

### Thumbtack
- **Standard signup:** No free credits included with direct signup
- **Through Contractor+ integration:** $100 in FREE leads when signing up through Contractor+ (contractorplus.app)
- **Thumbtack Promote:** Enables auto-respond to matching leads at 20% discount on credits
- **Free leads:** If no other contractor is available for a lead, you receive it for free (rare but happens)

### Bark.com
- **Standard signup:** No specific free credit offer currently advertised
- **Free lead viewing:** You see all matching lead details for free; only pay credits to respond
- **Bulk discounts:** Credits cost less when purchased in larger packs ($1.57/credit in bulk vs. $2.35 individually)
- **Important:** Credits expire 3 months after purchase

### PropStream (for finding leads, not receiving them)
- **Free trial:** 7-day free trial with 50 leads included
- **Use for:** Finding recent home buyers, property data, skip tracing
- **After trial:** Paid subscription required

### BuildZoom
- **Effectively free:** No upfront cost, no credits needed. Only pay 2.5% if actually hired.
- **This is the closest to "truly free" lead generation with no risk.**

### Porch.com
- **Vetted Pro trial:** Occasional promotions for new contractors. Check pro.porch.com for current offers.

## How to Maximize Free Trial Periods

1. **Sign up for all free profiles FIRST** (Google, Nextdoor, Yelp, Houzz, BuildZoom) before spending any money
2. **Use Contractor+ to sign up for Thumbtack** to get the $100 free leads offer
3. **Start with PropStream's 7-day trial** to pull recent home buyer data for Jacksonville, then cancel before it charges
4. **On Bark:** Buy the smallest credit pack first, test lead quality in your area before committing more
5. **Track EVERYTHING:** For every paid lead, record: platform, cost, whether they responded, whether you got hired, job revenue. This tells you exactly which platforms are worth paying for.
6. **Time your signups strategically:** Sign up for trial/credit platforms during spring (March-May) when home improvement demand peaks in Jacksonville

# PART 3: LOCAL OUTREACH SYSTEM -- JACKSONVILLE FL

## A) Finding and Reaching Local Contractors (Subcontract Partnership)

### Where to Find Them

**1. Google Maps**
- Search "general contractor Jacksonville FL," "plumber Jacksonville FL," "electrician Jacksonville FL," etc.
- Filter by ratings (4+ stars) and number of reviews
- Note: Busy contractors with lots of reviews are most likely to need overflow help

**2. Yelp**
- Search same categories as above
- Look for contractors with high review volume (indicates steady work flow)
- Check if they mention being "booked out" in their responses

**3. Better Business Bureau (BBB)**
- Go to bbb.org, search for contractors in Jacksonville, FL
- BBB-accredited contractors tend to be established businesses more likely to subcontract

**4. Florida DBPR License Lookup**
- Go to MyFloridaLicense.com
- Search by license type and location (Duval County / Jacksonville)
- This gives you a comprehensive list of licensed contractors in the area
- Useful for finding contractors who are not on Google/Yelp

**5. Jacksonville Contractor License Search**
- Go to jacksonville.gov/contractorlicensesearch
- Search the local Contrades database at contrades.coj.net
- Find all locally licensed contractors in the Jacksonville/Duval County area

**6. Local Trade Associations**
- Northeast Florida Builders Association (NEFBA): nefba.com
- Jacksonville Area Home Builders Association
- Local NARI chapter (National Association of the Remodeling Industry)
- Attend their meetings and networking events

### Cold Call Script for Contractors

**Opening:**
"Hi, this is [Your Name] with [Your Company]. I'm a licensed/insured handyman serving the Jacksonville area. I'm reaching out because I know busy contractors sometimes need reliable help with overflow work or smaller jobs that don't fit their schedule. Do you have 30 seconds?"

**If Yes:**
"I specialize in [drywall, painting, minor plumbing, general repairs, etc.]. I'm looking to build relationships with established contractors like yourself who might occasionally need a reliable subcontractor for:
- Smaller jobs that aren't worth your crew's time
- Overflow work when you're booked solid
- Punch list items on larger projects
- Quick-turnaround tenant turnover work

I'm licensed, insured, and I show up on time. Would it make sense to grab a quick coffee sometime so I can show you some of my work?"

**If They Say "We're Good":**
"Totally understand. Would you mind if I sent you my card? That way if something comes up down the road where you need backup, you have someone reliable to call."

### Cold Email Template for Contractors

**Subject:** Reliable Handyman Available for Overflow Work -- Jacksonville

**Body:**
Hi [Contractor Name],

I'm [Your Name], a licensed and insured handyman based in Jacksonville. I came across your business on [Google/Yelp/BBB] and I'm impressed by the work you do.

I'm reaching out because I'm building a network of local contractors who I can support with:

- Overflow work when your schedule is full
- Smaller jobs that may not justify sending a full crew
- Punch list and finishing work on larger projects
- Emergency/quick-turnaround repairs

I carry full liability insurance and [any relevant licenses]. I'm reliable, communicative, and focused on quality.

Would you be open to a quick 10-minute call or coffee to see if there's a fit? No pressure -- I'm just looking to be a resource when you need one.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Phone]
[Email]
[Website/Google Business Profile link]

### What to Offer Contractors
- **Overflow backup:** "Call me when you're too busy for a job"
- **Small job referral:** "Send me the $200-500 jobs you don't want"
- **Punch list specialist:** "I'll handle punch lists so your crew can start the next job"
- **Referral fee:** Offer 5-10% referral fee for jobs they send your way
- **Reciprocal referrals:** You refer larger jobs to them that are beyond your scope

### What Property Managers Need from a Handyman

**High-Frequency Needs:**
- Tenant turnover repairs (paint touch-ups, drywall patching, cleaning, minor fixes)
- On-call maintenance (leaky faucets, broken outlets, door repairs, appliance issues)

## C) Recent Home Buyers in Jacksonville (Past 90 Days)

### Why Target Recent Home Buyers
- New homeowners discover issues during move-in (leaky faucets, broken fixtures, scuffed walls)
- They want to customize their new home (new fixtures, painting, shelving, etc.)
- They don't have an established handyman yet -- you can become their go-to
- High lifetime value: if you do great work on the first job, they call you for years

### Where to Find Recent Home Buyer Data

**FREE Sources:**

**1. Duval County Property Appraiser**
- URL: duvalcountypropertyappraiser.org/property-search
- Search by sale date range, neighborhood, or address
- Sales history shows recent transfers with dates and prices
- 100% free, public record data
- Limitation: Manual searching, no bulk export

**2. Duval County Clerk of Court Official Records**
- URL: or.duvalclerk.com
- Search recorded deeds (warranty deeds indicate sales)
- Filter by recording date to find recent transactions
- Free to search, small fee for copies of documents

**3. Redfin Sold Listings**
- URL: redfin.com, search Jacksonville FL, filter "Sold" homes
- Shows recently sold homes with addresses, sale prices, and dates
- Free to browse
- Cannot bulk export, but you can manually compile lists
- Shows photos (helps estimate home condition and potential repair needs)

**4. Zillow Sold Listings**
- URL: zillow.com, search Jacksonville FL, filter "Recently Sold"
- Similar data to Redfin
- Includes estimated home values and neighborhood data

**5. Realtor.com Sold Listings**
- Similar functionality to Redfin and Zillow

**PAID Sources (For Bulk Data):**

**6. PropStream**
- URL: propstream.com
- 7-day free trial with 50 leads included
- Filter by: recent sale date, property type, location, price range
- Includes owner contact info (skip tracing)
- 165+ filters for precise targeting
- After trial: paid subscription (~$99/month)

**7. BatchLeads (Now owned by PropStream)**
- URL: batchleads.io
- Property data with skip tracing (phone numbers, emails)
- Direct mail integration
- Monthly lead packages from 10,000 to 150,000 contacts

**8. ListSource (by CoreLogic)**
- URL: listsource.com
- Purchase mailing lists filtered by: recent purchase date, location, home value
- Covers 134+ million properties nationwide
- Pay per list (costs vary by list size and filters)

**9. ATTOM Data / RealtyTrac**
- Property data and analytics
- Recent sales data with owner information
- Enterprise-level data for serious outreach campaigns

## Final Notes

### The 80/20 Rule for Lead Generation
80% of your leads will come from 20% of your effort. Based on this research, the highest-ROI activities are:
1. Google Business Profile optimization + review collection
2. Property manager outreach (recurring revenue)
3. Nextdoor engagement
4. Contractor partnership building
5. New homeowner direct mail

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. Paying for leads before optimizing free platforms
2. Spreading too thin across too many platforms
3. Not following up (80% of sales happen after the 5th contact)
4. Not asking every customer for a review
5. Generic outreach messages (always personalize)
6. Giving up too early (lead generation compounds over 3-6 months)
7. Not tracking costs and conversion rates

### Jacksonville-Specific Tips
- Peak season: March through October (warm weather = more home projects)
- Hurricane season (June-November): Offer storm prep and post-storm repair services
- Military market: Jacksonville has a large military population (NAS Jacksonville, Mayport). Market to military families who move frequently and need quick home services.
- Snowbird season: Part-time residents return October-April and often need home maintenance after being away.
- Growth areas: St. Johns County (south), Nassau County (north), Clay County (west) are rapidly growing -- expand service area as you grow.
