## 1. PLATFORM REALITY CHECK

### The Hard Truth
Facebook Marketplace officially PROHIBITS service-only listings. Their commerce policies state that listings must be for physical products, not services. Service listings that get reported are removed, and repeat offenders lose Marketplace access entirely.

### Why People Still Do It
Despite the prohibition, contractors across the US continue posting service-disguised-as-product listings because:
- Marketplace has 1.1 billion monthly users across 70 countries (as of March 2025)
- Local reach is unmatched -- buyers see listings within their zip code
- No advertising cost (unlike FB Ads which start at $5-10/day minimum)
- Home improvement activity spiked 31% year-over-year in early 2026
- Jacksonville FL has high demand with contractor waitlists exceeding 12 weeks in urban markets

### The Key Insight
The workaround is NOT to list a service. It is to list a TANGIBLE PRODUCT that happens to include a service component. The product must be real enough that if FB reviewed the listing, it could reasonably be called a product.

## 2. WHAT ACTUALLY WORKS (PRODUCT-STYLE WORKAROUNDS)

### Strategy A: The Gift Certificate / Voucher Method
List an actual printed gift certificate or service voucher as a product.
- You ARE selling a physical item (a printed certificate)
- The certificate happens to be redeemable for your services
- Price the certificate at the package value
- This is the SAFEST workaround because a gift certificate IS a product

**Example:** "Home Repair Gift Certificate - $150 Value" listed at $150
- Category: Home Goods or Classifieds
- You print an actual certificate, photograph it, list it
- When someone buys it, you show up and do the work

### Strategy B: The Kit / Package Method
Bundle a cheap physical product with "free" service included.
- List a physical item (inspection report binder, maintenance log, starter kit)
- Include "free installation" or "free consultation" with purchase
- The physical item is the product; the service is a bonus

**Example:** "Roof Inspection Report + FREE Repair Estimate" at $25
- The product is literally a printed roof inspection binder/folder
- The "bonus" is that you show up, inspect the roof, and provide the report in the binder
- Converts to paid roof repair jobs on-site

### Strategy C: The Maintenance Package / Subscription Box Method
List a "property maintenance package" as if it were a product box.
- Package includes a physical checklist, maintenance log, or branded folder
- The "product" is the documentation system
- Service visits are included as part of the "package"

**Example:** "Monthly Property Maintenance Package - Includes 4 Visits" at $199/month

### Strategy D: The "Miscellaneous" Category Play
Real listings exist in the Miscellaneous category under Classifieds for handyman services.
- A live listing was found: "Affordable Handyman & Lawn Care Services" posted under Miscellaneous in Lowell, Arkansas on FB Marketplace
- The Miscellaneous and Classifieds categories have looser enforcement
- These listings survive longer because the Classifieds section is treated more like Craigslist by Facebook

### Strategy E: The Free Listing Lead Magnet
List something genuinely free to generate leads.
- "FREE Home Maintenance Checklist - Jacksonville Area" at $0
- "FREE Wildlife Entry Point Assessment" at $0
- Free listings get HIGH visibility and rarely get flagged for being a "service"
- You collect the lead via Messenger, then convert to paid work

- Zero risk of being flagged for pricing issues
- Perfect for lead generation (free estimate, free inspection, free checklist)
- People message immediately because it is free

## 4. PRICING STRATEGY

### What Price Points Work

| Price | Risk Level | Conversion | Best Use |
|-------|-----------|------------|----------|
| **$0 (Free)** | LOWEST | Very High engagement, must convert in Messenger | Lead magnets, free estimates |
| **$1** | LOW | Looks suspicious; avoid | Not recommended |
| **$25** | LOW-MEDIUM | Good for consultation products | Inspection reports, assessments |
| **$50** | MEDIUM | Solid for gift certificates | Service vouchers |
| **$99-199** | MEDIUM | Package pricing, looks legitimate | Maintenance packages |
| **$150-500** | MEDIUM | Must match a real product value | Full service packages |

### Pricing Rules of Thumb
1. **$0 (Free)** is the safest and highest-engagement option. Use for lead generation.
2. **$1 is a red flag.** Facebook's algorithm flags $1 listings as potential spam or service disguises. Avoid it.
3. **$25-50** is the sweet spot for "consultation products" and small gift certificates.
4. **$99-299** works for packages that include a tangible deliverable (binder, report, checklist).
5. **Never list at exactly $0.01 or $0.99** -- these are spam signals.
6. **Match the price to what the physical product would reasonably cost.** A printed gift certificate at $150 makes sense. A "repair kit" at $500 does not.

## 5. LISTING STRUCTURE THAT CONVERTS

### Title Formula
**[Physical Product] + [Benefit/Location] + [Urgency or Qualifier]**

Good examples:
- "Roof Repair Assessment Kit - Jacksonville - Same Day Available"
- "Wildlife Removal Consultation Package - Duval County"
- "FREE Home Maintenance Checklist - Northeast FL"
- "Handyman Service Gift Certificate - $150 Value"

Bad examples (will get flagged):
- "Handyman Available - Call Now" (pure service, no product)
- "I Do Roof Repairs Cheap" (no product framing)
- "Wildlife Removal Service Jacksonville" (service, not product)

### Description Structure (5-Part Framework)

**Part 1 -- The Product (first 2 lines, visible in preview)**
Lead with what the physical product IS. This is what Facebook's review algorithm reads first.
> "Professional roof inspection report and assessment package for Jacksonville homeowners..."

**Part 2 -- What's Included**
List the physical items AND the service components as if they are all part of one package.
> "Package includes: Printed 12-point roof inspection report, Digital photo documentation, Written repair estimate, and complimentary on-site assessment..."

**Part 3 -- Local Credibility**
Mention Jacksonville neighborhoods, zip codes, or landmarks.
> "Serving all of Duval County including Arlington, Mandarin, Riverside, Beaches, and Westside..."

**Part 4 -- Call to Action**
Direct to Messenger. Never include a phone number in the listing body (against FB policies).
> "Message me for availability. Same-day and next-day appointments available."

**Part 5 -- Trust Signals**
Licensed, insured, years of experience, reviews.
> "Licensed and insured. 10+ years experience. 5-star rated."

### Photo Strategy

**DO use:**
- Professional-looking photos of the actual product (printed certificate, binder, kit)
- Before/after photos of completed work (these SELL)
- Photos of you in branded work gear at a job site
- Photos of your truck/van with business branding
- Clean, well-lit, high-resolution images (phone cameras are fine)

**DO NOT use:**
- Stock photos (Facebook can detect these and flag listings)
- Pure text images or flyers (flagged as ads)
- Photos with phone numbers or URLs overlaid (against policy)
- Logos only (looks like advertising, not a product listing)

**Photo count:** Upload 4-8 photos per listing. Facebook's algorithm boosts listings with more photos.

**Recommended photo lineup for each listing:**
1. The physical product (certificate, kit, binder) -- clean, staged
2. Before photo of a job
3. After photo of that same job
4. You or your team working on-site
5. Your branded vehicle or uniform
6. Another before/after pair
7. Close-up of quality workmanship
8. Happy customer (with permission) or finished result

## 7. RISK MITIGATION AND ACCOUNT SAFETY

### Posting Limits

| Account Age | Safe Daily Limit | Weekly Limit | Notes |
|-------------|-----------------|--------------|-------|
| Brand new (0-2 weeks) | 1 listing/day | 3-5/week | Go slow; Facebook restricts new accounts hard |
| 2-8 weeks old | 2-3 listings/day | 10-15/week | Build trust with legitimate product posts first |
| 2-6 months old | 3-5 listings/day | 15-25/week | Moderate risk; space out service-adjacent posts |
| 6+ months old | 5-10 listings/day | Up to 50/week | Established trust; still space out same-type posts |

### Spacing Rules
- Never post more than 1 service-adjacent listing per day from a new account
- Wait at least 4-6 hours between listings
- Mix in legitimate product sales (sell actual items you own) between service listings
- Post from the mobile app, not desktop (mobile-native listings get algorithmic priority as of 2024)
- Renew/bump existing listings instead of creating new duplicates

### What Gets Flagged
1. **Listing title contains "service" in a product category** -- almost instant flag
2. **Multiple nearly identical listings** -- treated as spam
3. **Phone numbers in listing photos or descriptions** -- against policy
4. **External URLs in descriptions** -- flagged
5. **Stock photos** -- Facebook can detect them
6. **Price of $0.01 or $1** -- spam signals
7. **Posting 5+ similar listings in one day** -- spam behavior flag
8. **Using words like "hire me" or "I'm available"** -- service language triggers

### What Stays Up
1. Listings framed as physical products with service as a bonus
2. Gift certificates (they ARE products)
3. Free listings offering something tangible (report, checklist, assessment)
4. Listings under Classifieds > Miscellaneous (less scrutiny)
5. Listings with real, original photos (not stock, not text overlays)
6. Listings that naturally look like someone selling a product

### When a Listing Gets Removed
1. DO NOT immediately repost the same listing -- this escalates flags on your account
2. Wait 48-72 hours before posting anything similar
3. Rewrite the listing with different title, description, and photos
4. Consider switching categories (if Home Improvement got flagged, try Classifieds)
5. If you get a warning, post only legitimate product sales for 1-2 weeks before trying again
6. Appeal the removal through Facebook's review process (sometimes listings are restored)

### Multiple Accounts Strategy

**Risks:**
- Facebook detects multiple accounts from the same device/IP/browser fingerprint
- If one account is banned and linked to another, both can be banned
- Violates Facebook Terms of Service

**If you do it anyway (not recommended):**
- Use separate devices (different phones)
- Use separate phone numbers for verification
- Use separate email addresses
- Never log into both from the same WiFi network
- Each account should have a distinct posting pattern and history
- Build each account with normal social activity first (30+ days of regular use)

**Better alternative:** Use ONE well-maintained personal account and supplement with your Facebook Business Page posts, local group posts, and paid FB Ads.

### Account Warming Strategy (For New or Inactive Accounts)
1. **Week 1-2:** Post 1-2 legitimate products you actually own for sale (furniture, electronics, etc.)
2. **Week 3:** Post 1 free item and 1 low-priced item
3. **Week 4:** Post your first service-adjacent listing (gift certificate or free assessment)
4. **Week 5+:** Gradually increase to 2-3 service-adjacent listings per week
5. **Ongoing:** Always maintain a mix of at least 50% legitimate product listings

## 8. COMMUNITY INTELLIGENCE (REDDIT / FORUMS)

### What Contractors Report Working

Based on research across Quora, BlackHatWorld, and contractor forums:

**Consensus findings:**
- Service listings ARE against Marketplace policy and WILL eventually be removed if reported
- The Miscellaneous/Classifieds category has the longest survival time for service-adjacent listings
- "Gift certificate" framing is the most commonly recommended workaround
- Free listings generate the most leads with the least risk
- Listings survive longer if they receive quick engagement (messages, saves)
- Listings posted from mobile app survive longer than desktop-created listings
- Aged accounts (6+ months of normal Marketplace activity) have far fewer removals

**Common mistakes contractors report:**
- Posting the same listing in multiple categories simultaneously (instant spam flag)
- Using the word "service" in the title within product categories
- Including phone numbers in photos (use Messenger only)
- Posting too many listings at once when they first discover Marketplace
- Not responding to messages quickly (low response rate hurts future listing visibility)

**BlackHatWorld insights on listing longevity:**
- New accounts face the tightest restrictions; warm up with legitimate sales first
- Listings every 48-72 hours is safer than daily
- Account age and positive selling history are the biggest factors in what stays up
- Listings that get quick engagement (within the first hour) get boosted by the algorithm and are less likely to be reviewed

## 9. ALTERNATIVE LEAD CHANNELS ON FACEBOOK (COMPLEMENT MARKETPLACE)

### Use These ALONGSIDE Marketplace Listings

**1. Facebook Local Buy/Sell Groups (HIGHEST VALUE)**
- Join 10-20 Jacksonville buy/sell and neighborhood groups
- Groups like "Jacksonville FL Buy Sell Trade" and neighborhood-specific groups
- Many allow service providers to post directly (check group rules)
- No algorithm restrictions -- your post shows up in the group feed
- This is where MOST successful contractor leads come from on Facebook

**2. Facebook Business Page**
- Create a professional Business Page for your handyman operation
- Post before/after content regularly
- Pin your best offer to the top
- Use Facebook's "Get Quote" or "Message" call-to-action buttons
- Boost individual posts for $5-10/day to reach Jacksonville residents

**3. Facebook Neighborhood Groups (via Nextdoor-style features)**
- Facebook has local community features similar to Nextdoor
- Neighbors asking for contractor recommendations is extremely common
- Being active and helpful in these groups generates organic referrals

**4. Facebook Paid Ads (Marketplace Placement)**
- You CAN advertise services through Facebook Ads and select Marketplace as a placement
- This is the OFFICIAL way to get service listings on Marketplace
- Costs money ($5-20/day) but zero risk of account suspension
- Target Jacksonville zip codes specifically
- Start with $5/day and scale what works

**5. Commenting Strategy**
- Search Jacksonville groups for posts like "looking for a handyman" or "need roof repair"
- Comment with a helpful response and your page link
- Be helpful first, salesy second
- Set up Facebook notifications for keywords in your local groups
