## Service Packages

### "Honey-Do List" Package
- **What:** Customer gives a list of 3-5 small tasks, we knock them all out in one visit
- **Pricing:** $299-499 flat rate (2-4 hour visit)
- **Best for:** Busy homeowners, elderly, property managers
- **Margin:** High — tasks are quick, batch efficiency

### "Seasonal Maintenance" Package
- **Spring:** Gutter clean, pressure wash, deck inspection, AC filter change
- **Fall:** Gutter clean, weatherstripping, furnace filter, exterior inspection
- **Pricing:** $199-349 per visit
- **Best for:** Recurring revenue, subscription model potential

### "Move-In / Move-Out" Package
- **What:** TV mounting, furniture assembly, blind installation, touch-up paint, deep clean coordination
- **Pricing:** $399-799 depending on scope
- **Best for:** Realtors, property managers, new homeowners

### "Property Manager Retainer"
- **What:** On-call handyman for a portfolio of rental properties
- **Pricing:** $500-1500/month retainer + per-job billing for larger tasks
- **Best for:** Property managers with 10+ units
- **Why it works:** Guaranteed recurring revenue, priority scheduling

## Pricing Strategy

### Hourly Rate
- **Standard:** $65-95/hour (market dependent)
- **After-hours/weekend:** $95-125/hour
- **Minimum charge:** 1 hour or $75 (whichever is higher)

### Flat Rate (Recommended for Most Jobs)
- Customers prefer knowing the price upfront
- Build in 20-30% margin above estimated time + materials
- Always quote a range, close at the midpoint

### Materials Markup
- Standard: 15-25% markup on materials
- If buying from Home Depot with Pro Xtra: factor in points earned

### Payment
- 50% deposit on jobs over $500
- Accept: Cash, Venmo, Zelle, card (Square/Stripe)
- Net-30 for property manager retainers only

## Licensing Notes

**Check your specific state and municipality.** General rules:
- Most states allow unlicensed handyman work up to $500-1000 per job
- Plumbing, electrical, HVAC, and structural work almost always require licenses
- General contractor license often needed for jobs over your state's threshold
- Always carry general liability insurance ($1M minimum)
- Workers' comp required if you have employees (varies by state)
