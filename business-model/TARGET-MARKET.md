# Target Market — Handyman & Home Services

## Primary Customer Segments

### 1. Busy Dual-Income Homeowners (Largest Segment)
- **Demographics:** 30-55, household income $80K-200K, own home
- **Pain:** No time for home maintenance, weekend is sacred
- **Behavior:** Google "handyman near me", check reviews, want fast booking
- **What they buy:** Honey-do lists, quick fixes, seasonal maintenance
- **Average job:** $150-400
- **Lifetime value:** $1,500-5,000/year if recurring
- **Where to find them:** Google, Nextdoor, Facebook groups, neighborhood apps
- **Key trigger:** Something breaks, or spouse creates a task list

### 2. Property Managers / Landlords (Highest LTV)
- **Demographics:** Manage 5-100+ rental units
- **Pain:** Need reliable, on-call maintenance at predictable cost
- **Behavior:** Word of mouth, referrals, want consistency over lowest price
- **What they buy:** Retainer packages, turn maintenance, emergency repairs
- **Average job:** $200-600 per call
- **Lifetime value:** $6,000-18,000/year on retainer
- **Where to find them:** Real estate meetups, BiggerPockets, LinkedIn, cold email
- **Key trigger:** Current handyman flaked, scaling portfolio

### 3. Elderly / Aging-in-Place Homeowners
- **Demographics:** 65+, fixed income but owns home outright
- **Pain:** Can't physically do maintenance, need someone trustworthy
- **Behavior:** Referrals from family/neighbors, Nextdoor, church/community
- **What they buy:** Small repairs, safety upgrades (grab bars, smoke detectors), seasonal maintenance
- **Average job:** $100-250
- **Lifetime value:** $1,200-3,000/year
- **Where to find them:** Nextdoor, senior centers, church bulletins, Area Agency on Aging
- **Key trigger:** Family member notices something needs fixing

### 4. Real Estate Agents (Referral Channel)
- **Demographics:** Active listing agents in your service area
- **Pain:** Need fast turnaround on pre-listing repairs and move-in prep
- **Behavior:** Referral-based, need speed and reliability above all
- **What they buy:** Pre-listing repairs, staging prep, move-in/move-out packages
- **Average job:** $300-800
- **Lifetime value:** $3,000-10,000/year (repeat referrals)
- **Where to find them:** Open houses, local Realtor associations, Facebook Realtor groups
- **Key trigger:** Listing with issues that need fixing before photos

### 5. Small Business / Commercial (Growth Segment)
- **Demographics:** Small retail, restaurants, offices, churches
- **Pain:** Building maintenance needs, don't justify a full-time person
- **Behavior:** Want a reliable go-to person, often negotiate monthly rates
- **What they buy:** Bathroom repairs, painting, fixture replacement, general upkeep
- **Average job:** $200-500
- **Lifetime value:** $4,000-12,000/year
- **Where to find them:** Door-to-door, cold email, local business associations
- **Key trigger:** Something visible breaks (customer-facing)

## Geographic Targeting

### Ideal Service Area
- **Radius:** 15-25 miles from home base (drives further = less profit)
- **Sweet spots:** Suburban neighborhoods built 10-30 years ago (constant maintenance needs)
- **Avoid:** Brand-new construction (warranty covers everything) and very rural areas (drive time kills margins)

### Neighborhood Prioritization
| Priority | Neighborhood Type | Why |
|----------|------------------|-----|
| A | Established suburbs, $300K-600K homes, 10-25 years old | High maintenance needs, homeowners can afford it |
| A | Rental-heavy areas near universities/downtown | Property managers need constant turns |
| B | Upscale neighborhoods, $600K+ homes | Higher job values but more competition, pickier customers |
| B | 55+ communities / retirement neighborhoods | Consistent small job demand, trust-based |
| C | Newer suburbs (< 5 years old) | Less maintenance needed, wait for aging |
| C | Rural areas | Drive time kills margins |

## Competitive Positioning

### What You're Competing Against
1. **Other handymen** — unreliable, don't answer phone, no-show
2. **Big franchises** (Mr. Handyman, Handyman Connection) — expensive, impersonal
3. **DIY / YouTube** — customer tried and failed, now calling you
4. **Licensed specialists** — overkill and expensive for small jobs
5. **Task apps** (TaskRabbit, Handy) — inconsistent quality, platform takes cut

### Your Differentiators
- **Speed:** Same-day or next-day for small jobs
- **Reliability:** Show up on time, every time, communicate proactively
- **Transparency:** Flat-rate pricing, no surprise charges
- **Technology:** Online booking, text updates, digital invoicing
- **Reviews:** 4.8+ on Google, photos of every job
- **Convenience:** "Honey-do list" package — batch everything into one visit

### Positioning Statement
> "The reliable handyman who actually shows up. Same-day service, flat-rate pricing, 5-star reviews. Text us your list, we'll handle the rest."
