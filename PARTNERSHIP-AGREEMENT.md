## What This Is

This is a working agreement between two people starting a handyman and property maintenance business together on the side. We're both putting in time around our existing work. The goal is to build this into a full-time operation.

This is an LLC-to-LLC agreement. We are not forming a new entity together at this time. If and when we hit consistent revenue ($10K+/month), we'll revisit forming a joint entity.

---

## The Business

**Services offered:**
1. Wildlife Removal — trapping, exclusion, attic cleanup
2. Roof Repair — leak fixes, shingle replacement, storm damage, flashing
3. Handyman Work — drywall, fixtures, painting, doors, trim, general repairs
4. On-Call Property Maintenance — lawn care, pressure washing, gutter cleaning, turnover prep, move-out cleanups

**Target market:** Jacksonville, FL — homeowners, landlords, property managers, investors, commercial property managers

**Price range:** $150–$5,000 per job (staying under the threshold that requires a general contractor license)

## Who Does What

### Justin's Responsibilities
- **Marketing & Lead Generation:** Set up and manage all advertising — Craigslist, Facebook Marketplace, Nextdoor, Porch, Google Business Profile, free classified sites, and any other platforms
- **AI & Automation:** Build and maintain AI phone receptionist, automated inbox monitoring (FB Marketplace every 5 minutes), Craigslist posting automation, email campaigns
- **Outreach:** Set up outreach to local property managers, contractors (for subcontract work), and recent home buyers (past 90 days from county records)
- **Operations Backend:** Set up systems to manage leads, jobs, scheduling, and team coordination
- **Content & Branding:** Help create ad copy, social media content, and marketing materials from job site videos and photos
- **Social SDR System:** Automated social selling across platforms
- **Technology costs:** Justin covers his own technology stack (AI tools, APIs, software) — these are not charged to the business

### Maudi's Responsibilities
- **Field Work:** Perform the actual jobs, especially in the early phase
- **Team Building:** Hire, train, and manage field workers as the business grows. Goal is to get Maudi out of doing the physical work and into a supervisor/estimator role as soon as possible
- **Quality Control:** Oversee job quality, handle on-site issues, ensure customer satisfaction
- **Quotes & Estimates:** Visit job sites, assess work needed, provide quotes to customers
- **Equipment & Vehicle:** Provide truck and tools for jobs
- **Licensing & Insurance:** Maintain current business insurance and any required licenses/permits
- **Marketing Budget:** Cover the monthly marketing/advertising spend from business revenue (or out of pocket until revenue covers it)

## The Numbers

### Revenue Split: 35% / 65%

**Justin receives 35% of net profit.**
**Maudi receives 65% of net profit.**

**Net profit is calculated as:**
> Total revenue collected
> MINUS marketing/advertising expenses
> MINUS materials cost for jobs
> MINUS worker salaries/pay (subcontractors, employees, day labor)
> MINUS business insurance
> MINUS any other direct business expenses (permits, dump fees, equipment rentals, etc.)
> = **Net profit**, which is then split 35/65

**What is NOT deducted before the split:**
- Justin's personal technology costs (AI tools, APIs, software subscriptions) — Justin covers these himself
- Maudi's personal vehicle costs (fuel, maintenance on his personal truck) — unless we agree otherwise
- Personal expenses of either party

### Payout Schedule
- Paid out **every two weeks** (biweekly)
- We'll track revenue and expenses in a shared spreadsheet or system
- Both parties can see all numbers at all times — full transparency

### Marketing Budget
- **Starting budget:** $500–$1,000 per month
- As the business grows, we **reinvest 10% of gross revenue** back into marketing to maintain a healthy pipeline
- Marketing expenses are paid from business revenue first. If revenue hasn't caught up yet (early days), Maudi covers the marketing spend out of pocket and it's deducted before the profit split
- Justin will communicate what needs to be paid for and get agreement before spending

### Budget Transparency
- Justin will provide a clear breakdown of what each marketing dollar is spent on
- Major expenses (anything over $200 in a single transaction) require a heads-up to Maudi first
- We'll use Privacy.com virtual cards or a similar system so Maudi can control and monitor spend without handing over personal card info

## The Ground Rules

### Communication
- We keep it real with each other. If something's not working, we say it
- Quick daily check-in (text or 5-min call) during active weeks
- Longer weekly call to review numbers and plan the week ahead

### Decision Making
- **Marketing & lead gen decisions:** Justin leads, keeps Maudi informed
- **Field operations & hiring decisions:** Maudi leads, keeps Justin informed
- **Major business decisions** (anything over $1,000, new service lines, partnerships): Both agree

### What If It Doesn't Work Out
- **30-day test period:** Either party can walk away at the 30-day mark. No penalties, no hard feelings
- **After 30 days:** Either party can exit with 14 days written notice
- **If Justin exits:** Maudi keeps the business, all accounts, and all customer relationships. Justin transfers any business-specific accounts (Craigslist, Google Business Profile, etc.) to Maudi. Justin removes his automation systems within 14 days
- **If Maudi exits:** Justin keeps any marketing systems and templates he built (for use in other ventures). Business accounts stay with Maudi's LLC since they're under his name
- **No non-compete:** Neither party is restricted from working in home services, handyman, or any related field after parting ways
- **Outstanding payments:** Any revenue earned before the exit date is still split 35/65 and paid out on the normal schedule

### Intellectual Property
- Marketing systems, AI tools, and automation that Justin builds: Justin owns these (he uses them across multiple ventures)
- Business name, brand, customer list, and relationships: Owned by Maudi's LLC
- Ad copy, templates, and content created specifically for this business: Shared — both can use
