## Research Notes - 2025/2026 Current State

## 1. HOME DEPOT PRO REFERRAL

### How It Works
- Contractors create a profile on proreferral.homedepot.com (takes ~15 minutes)
- When Home Depot shoppers (online or in-store) search for a local pro, they are matched with your profile
- Leads are sent directly to you -- no commission on jobs

### Cost Structure
- **No direct cash cost per lead.** Uses a points-based system tied to the Pro Xtra loyalty program
- Every $1 spent at Home Depot = 2 Pro Xtra points
- Each referral/lead costs 40-80 points depending on service type
- No commission fees on completed jobs
- Effectively "free" leads if you already buy materials at Home Depot

### Requirements
- Proof of General Liability insurance
- Contractor license (as required by your state)
- Personal ID with photo (driver's license or passport)
- Pro Xtra account (free)

### Lead Quality Rating: 6/10
- Leads come from homeowners already shopping for materials, so intent is moderate-to-high
- Volume is generally LOW compared to dedicated platforms
- Better for trades closely tied to Home Depot products (painting, flooring, plumbing, electrical)

### Volume Potential: LOW
- This is a supplementary channel, not a primary lead source
- Volume depends on local Home Depot traffic and your category

### Effort to Maintain: LOW
- Set up profile, keep insurance current, that's about it
- Mobile app available to manage leads

### Worth It? YES as a free add-on
- Zero-risk since there is no cash outlay (just points from purchases you would make anyway)
- Not a primary lead source but easy passive leads
- Best for contractors who already spend heavily at Home Depot

## 2. PORCH.COM

### How It Works
- Contractors create a profile and set service area/categories
- Porch sends shared leads based on homeowner requests
- Integrated with Lowe's as a retail partner (adds credibility and lead volume)
- Offers project management tools and financing options for contractors

### Cost Structure
- **Pay As You Go:** $5-$100 per shared lead (varies by service type and region)
- Most leads fall in the $10-$60 range
- **Vetted Pro Program:** Additional $360/year for premium badge
- Auto-credit if you cannot connect with a lead after 2 calls + 2 messages with no response
- No monthly subscription required for basic participation

### Lead Quality Rating: 5/10
- Leads described as "well-qualified" by the platform due to homeowner readiness
- However, BBB complaints cite worthless leads, disconnected numbers, consumers not interested
- Mixed -- the Lowe's integration helps quality somewhat but shared leads mean competition

### Volume Potential: MEDIUM
- Lowe's partnership provides a significant lead funnel
- Volume varies heavily by market and trade category

### Effort to Maintain: LOW-MEDIUM
- Respond quickly to leads (speed matters for shared leads)
- Keep profile updated
- Manage credits/disputes for bad leads

### Worth It? MAYBE -- test first
- Better than some alternatives due to Lowe's integration
- The credit-back policy for unreachable leads is a nice safety net
- Best for contractors who want pay-per-lead without subscription commitments
- Watch your actual conversion rate closely and calculate true cost per acquisition

## 3. FACEBOOK MARKETPLACE FOR SERVICES

### How It Works
- Facebook Marketplace officially only allows tangible physical items
- Services are technically NOT allowed in Marketplace listings
- HOWEVER, workarounds exist: list as "Item for sale" and describe as a service
- The real play is Facebook Business Pages + Facebook Ads, not Marketplace itself
- Facebook's home services directory (populated via partners like Handy, HomeAdvisor, Porch) lists 100,000+ providers

### Cost Structure
- **Marketplace listings:** Free (but against TOS for services -- risk of removal)
- **Facebook Ads (the real strategy):** $5-$50/day budgets common for local contractors
- Cost per lead via Facebook Ads: typically $15-$50 for home services
- **Business Page:** Free to create and maintain

### The Real Strategy for 2025-2026
1. Create a strong Facebook Business Page with services section and "Book Now" button
2. Post before/after photos, time-lapse videos, day-in-the-life content
3. Run hyper-local Facebook Ads targeting specific neighborhoods (not whole cities)
4. Use Lead Form ads for easy capture
5. Short-form video is the top-performing content format
6. Retarget website visitors
7. Join and actively participate in local community Facebook Groups
8. Authentic, unedited content outperforms polished marketing

### Lead Quality Rating: 6/10
- Quality depends entirely on your targeting and ad creative
- Community group leads tend to be higher quality (warm referrals)
- Cold ad leads require nurturing

### Volume Potential: HIGH (with ad spend)
- Massive audience -- nearly everyone is on Facebook
- Can scale up or down with budget
- Local groups can provide steady organic leads with effort

### Effort to Maintain: HIGH
- Requires consistent content creation
- Ad management and optimization
- Community group participation
- Responding to messages/comments quickly

### Worth It? YES -- but as Facebook Ads + Page, NOT Marketplace
- One of the best ROI channels when done right
- Requires learning or hiring for ad management
- Organic community group participation is free and effective
- Do NOT rely on Marketplace listings for services (against TOS and unreliable)

## 4. NEXTDOOR

### How It Works
- Verified neighborhood-based social network
- Free Business Page available to any local business
- 2 free posts per month to neighbors' newsfeeds
- Paid options: Neighborhood Sponsorships and Nextdoor Ads
- Neighbors can recommend businesses, creating organic social proof
- "Faves" program -- annual neighbor voting for best local businesses

### Cost Structure
- **Free tier:** Business Page + 2 posts/month + ability to receive recommendations
- **Nextdoor Ads:** Starting at $1/day budget; CPC ranges $0.50-$2.00
- **Local Deals:** $3-$90 depending on region and targeting
- **Neighborhood Sponsorships:** Pricing varies by zip code (based on residents and housing cost)
- No per-lead fee -- it is ad-spend based

### Key Stats
- 77% of Nextdoor users are homeowners
- Users are 161% more likely to be interested in remodeling
- 141% more likely to care about home improvement
- One business reported 44% lead close rates from Nextdoor
- In 2025, neighbors cast 6.9 million Faves for 1.7 million businesses

### Lead Quality Rating: 8/10
- Highest quality leads of any social platform for home services
- Homeowner-dense, trust-based community
- Recommendations from neighbors carry enormous weight
- Leads come with built-in social proof

### Volume Potential: LOW-MEDIUM
- Lower volume than Facebook but MUCH higher quality
- Volume depends on neighborhood density and activity
- Organic reach is limited to 2 posts/month without paying

### Effort to Maintain: LOW-MEDIUM
- Claim and optimize Business Page
- Post 2x/month (free)
- Respond to neighbor questions and recommendations
- Optionally run ads/sponsorships
- Encourage satisfied customers to recommend you on Nextdoor

### Worth It? STRONGLY YES
- Best kept secret for home services lead gen
- The trust factor is unmatched -- neighbors recommending you to neighbors
- Free tier alone can generate quality leads
- Paid ads are affordable and highly targeted
- Essential platform for any local home services business

## 5. THUMBTACK

### How It Works
- Contractors create a profile with services, pricing, service area
- Homeowners submit project requests
- Thumbtack matches requests to pros and charges for leads
- Dynamic pricing -- costs change weekly based on supply/demand
- Leads are shared (sent to multiple contractors)

### Cost Structure
- **Cost per lead:** $10-$170+ (wide range based on trade, job size, location, competition)
- Average leads tend to be $50-$100 range for most trades
- Uses "credits" system -- buy credits, spend them to contact leads
- Pricing updates weekly based on market conditions
- No monthly subscription required
- "Instant Match" feature can auto-respond to leads (costs more)

### Lead Quality Rating: 3/10
- Widely criticized in 2025-2026 contractor reviews
- Common complaints: fake leads, disconnected numbers, tire-kickers, people who never heard of Thumbtack
- Same lead sent to multiple contractors creates bidding war
- Reported conversion rate: 10-30% (and many contractors say closer to 10%)
- One contractor paid $140 for 8 leads, only 1 converted

### Volume Potential: MEDIUM-HIGH
- Large consumer-facing platform with significant traffic
- Lots of leads available -- but quality is the issue
- Good volume in urban/suburban markets

### Effort to Maintain: MEDIUM
- Profile optimization matters
- Speed of response is critical (first responder advantage)
- Need to actively manage budget and lead selection
- Dispute bad leads for credits back

### Worth It? DECLINING -- use as supplement only
- Was better 3-5 years ago; quality has deteriorated significantly
- Rising prices + declining quality = bad trend
- Can work as a SUPPLEMENTARY channel during slow periods
- Best for newer contractors building initial reviews/reputation
- Do NOT rely on Thumbtack as primary lead source
- Calculate your actual cost per BOOKED job, not just per lead

## 6. ANGI / HOMEADVISOR

### How It Works
- Angi acquired HomeAdvisor; they now operate as one platform (Angi Leads)
- Contractors pay an annual fee + per-lead charges
- Leads are shared among 3-8 contractors simultaneously
- Homeowners submit project requests; Angi sends to matching contractors
- Various advertising tiers available

### Cost Structure
- **Annual subscription:** ~$288-$350/year
- **Per lead:** $25-$120 depending on trade and market
- HVAC, roofing, remodeling leads at the higher end
- **Effective cost per BOOKED JOB:** Often exceeds $1,400 according to contractor reports
- One-year contracts common with early termination fees
- Additional advertising spend available (and heavily pushed by sales reps)

### Lead Quality Rating: 3/10
- Conversion rates: HomeAdvisor ~28%, Angi ~24% (on the lead itself, not booking)
- Leads shared with 3-8 other contractors
- Frequent complaints about lead quality, fake leads, unresponsive consumers
- Multiple lawsuits over questionable leads and review practices
- The subscription + per-lead model means you pay even when leads are bad

### Volume Potential: MEDIUM-HIGH
- Large platform with significant consumer traffic
- Lots of leads available, but shared leads reduce effective win rate

### Effort to Maintain: MEDIUM-HIGH
- Manage subscription and lead spend
- Respond quickly to compete with other contractors receiving same lead
- Deal with aggressive upsell calls from Angi sales team
- Contract cancellation can be difficult and expensive
- Need to actively dispute bad leads

### Worth It? GENERALLY NO for established businesses
- The math rarely works: $1,400+ per booked job is unsustainable for most trades
- Shared lead model is fundamentally flawed for contractors
- Aggressive contracts and upselling
- May be useful ONLY for brand-new contractors with zero online presence
- Most contractors who have tried it recommend leaving
- Better alternatives exist (LSA, direct marketing, SEO)

## 7. GOOGLE LOCAL SERVICES ADS (LSA)

### How It Works
- Ads appear at the very top of Google search results (above regular Google Ads)
- "Google Guaranteed" or "Google Screened" badge displayed
- Pay per lead (phone call or message), NOT per click
- Requires background check, insurance verification, license verification
- Leads are semi-exclusive (sent to fewer contractors than Angi/Thumbtack)

### Cost Structure
- **Cost per lead by trade (2026 data):**
  - Plumbing: $35-$69
  - HVAC: $45-$80 (emergency calls can spike to $100+)
  - Electrical: $35-$70
  - Painting: ~$40
  - General range: $20-$85 depending on trade and market
- **Recommended weekly budget:** $300-$1,200 depending on trade/market/goals
- Pay only when customer contacts you through the ad
- Can dispute invalid leads for refund
- LSA costs have climbed ~40% in competitive markets since 2023

### Lead Quality Rating: 9/10
- HIGHEST quality leads of any paid platform
- Conversion rate: 31% lead-to-customer (vs. 12% for traditional PPC)
- Customers are actively searching with intent to hire NOW
- Semi-exclusive leads (less competition than Angi/Thumbtack)
- Google Guaranteed badge builds instant trust

### Volume Potential: MEDIUM-HIGH
- ~70% of contractors now run LSAs (up from 28% in 2021), so competition is increasing
- Volume depends on search demand in your area
- Seasonal fluctuations (HVAC peaks in summer/winter, etc.)

### Effort to Maintain: MEDIUM
- Initial setup requires verification (background check, insurance, license) -- can take 2-4 weeks
- Ongoing: manage budget, respond to leads within 5 minutes, collect Google reviews
- Reviews are the #1 optimization lever -- more 5-star reviews = more leads at lower cost
- Dispute invalid leads regularly

### Key Optimization Tips
- Respond within 5 MINUTES (first callback wins)
- Aggressively collect Google reviews (quantity and recency matter most)
- Set appropriate budget -- too low and you miss leads during peak hours
- Dispute every bad lead (non-service-area, spam, wrong service)
- Google Guaranteed money-back guarantee was discontinued Nov 7, 2025 (badge still shows)

### Worth It? STRONGLY YES -- #1 recommended platform
- Best cost per booked job of any platform (~$250 per closed job at 30% close rate)
- Highest intent leads -- people searching Google are ready to hire
- Should be PRIMARY lead channel for most home services contractors
- Rising competition means you must optimize (reviews, response time, budget)
- Only downside: requires passing Google's verification process

## 8. YELP FOR CONTRACTORS

### How It Works
- Free business listing available to all contractors
- Paid advertising available (Yelp Ads) to boost visibility
- Review-driven platform -- your star rating is everything
- Yelp's algorithm filters reviews (controversial -- sometimes hides legitimate reviews)

### Cost Structure
- **Free listing:** $0 -- create and optimize profile, respond to reviews
- **Yelp Ads CPC:** $0.30-$40+ per click (higher than Google Ads for most contractors)
- Actual reported CPC: ~$8-$20 per click
- **Yelp Enhanced Profile:** $1-$5/day for enhanced listing features
- Monthly ad budgets typically $300-$1,000+ for meaningful results
- Yelp offers initial ad credit coupons -- use these to test

### Lead Quality Rating: 5/10
- Yelp users often contact 4-5 businesses simultaneously (comparison shoppers)
- Higher quality in urban areas where Yelp usage is strong
- Works best for high-ticket services where one conversion covers monthly ad spend
- Organic (free) leads from good reviews are higher quality than paid ad leads

### Volume Potential: MARKET DEPENDENT
- Strong in major metros (SF, NYC, LA, Chicago)
- Weak to moderate in suburban/rural markets
- Declining overall as Google and social platforms dominate local search

### Effort to Maintain: LOW (free) / MEDIUM (paid)
- Free: Set up profile, respond to reviews, encourage happy customers to review
- Paid: Manage ad budget, track conversions, adjust targeting
- Review management is critical -- respond to every review (positive and negative)

### Worth It?
- **Free listing: YES, absolutely.** No reason not to have one
- **Paid advertising: ONLY in strong Yelp markets (major cities) and for high-ticket services**
- Platform is getting saturated; ROI declining in many markets
- If you are in a major city doing $5,000+ jobs, paid Yelp can work
- If you are suburban/rural or doing small jobs, skip paid Yelp
- ALWAYS try the free ad credit coupon first before committing

## 9. HOUZZ

### How It Works
- Visual portfolio/inspiration platform for home design and renovation
- Contractors create a profile showcasing projects with photos
- Houzz Pro is the paid software suite (project management, CRM, lead gen)
- Homeowners browse for inspiration and can contact pros directly
- Ideabooks, project photos, and Q&A features

### Cost Structure
- **Free profile:** Available -- showcase projects, receive organic inquiries
- **Houzz Pro Essential (designers):** $149/month
- **Houzz Pro (contractors):** $249/month
- **Lead generation advertising add-on:** Starting at $499/month (SEPARATE from Houzz Pro subscription)
- Flat-rate pricing (no per-lead fees)
- 12-month contracts reported by some users (lock-in)
- Pricing has reportedly tripled in the last 2 years

### Lead Quality Rating: 4/10 (for lead gen) / 7/10 (for branding/portfolio)
- Lead generation quality described as "awful" by many contractors
- Leads often seek handyman services rather than specialized work
- Many leads do not respond
- HOWEVER, as a portfolio/branding platform, Houzz is excellent for high-end work
- Homeowners on Houzz have higher budgets (median renovation spend: $20,000)
- 93% of homeowners plan to work with professionals in 2026

### Volume Potential: LOW
- Not a high-volume lead platform
- Better for attracting high-value projects organically
- Paid lead gen produces disappointing volume for the cost

### Effort to Maintain: MEDIUM
- Upload high-quality project photos regularly
- Maintain portfolio with detailed descriptions
- Respond to Q&A questions in your category
- Manage Houzz Pro tools if subscribed

### Worth It?
- **Free profile for portfolio/branding: YES** (especially for renovation, design, remodeling)
- **Houzz Pro subscription: MAYBE** if you need the project management tools
- **Paid lead generation add-on: GENERALLY NO** -- too expensive for the volume and quality
- Best suited for higher-end contractors (remodelers, designers, custom builders, kitchen/bath)
- Think of Houzz as a BRANDING tool, not a lead gen tool
- Your beautiful project photos on Houzz help close deals from OTHER lead sources

## 10. OTHER PLATFORMS: BUILDZOOM, BARK, TASKRABBIT

### BuildZoom

**How It Works:**
- Matches contractors with homeowners using public permit and licensing data
- You browse and meet with clients for FREE
- Only pay a referral fee if you WIN the job

**Cost Structure:**
- Free to join, free to receive leads, free to meet clients
- 2.5% commission on completed jobs (ONLY pay if hired)
- No upfront costs

**Lead Quality Rating: 7/10**
- Higher quality leads because they use permit data to match expertise
- Homeowners who use BuildZoom tend to be more serious (bigger projects)
- Lower volume but better fit

**Volume Potential: LOW**
- Smaller platform, less consumer awareness
- Best in markets where BuildZoom has traction

**Effort to Maintain: LOW**
- Profile setup, respond to leads

**Worth It? YES as a no-risk add-on**
- Zero upfront cost, pay only if you get hired
- Best for larger project contractors (remodels, additions, new construction)
- The 2.5% commission model is the most contractor-friendly in the industry

---

### Bark

**How It Works:**
- Homeowners post service requests
- Contractors buy credits to respond to leads they choose
- You select which leads to pursue (not auto-sent)

**Cost Structure:**
- Credit-based system: Standard credit = $2.35 each
- Leads cost varying numbers of credits (based on job value, service type, demand)
- Effective cost per lead: $5-$50+ depending on service
- Elite Pro Subscription: 5 free responses/month + 20% credit discount
- Credits expire 3 months after purchase (as of Nov 2025)
- Money-back guarantee on first credit pack if you don't win business

**Lead Quality Rating: 5/10**
- Quality varies significantly
- Strong competition for each lead
- You choose which leads to pursue (good for budget control)

**Volume Potential: LOW-MEDIUM**
- Growing platform but smaller than Thumbtack/Angi
- Better presence in some markets than others

**Effort to Maintain: LOW-MEDIUM**
- Browse leads and choose which to respond to
- Manage credit balance

**Worth It? MAYBE -- low risk to test**
- The choose-your-leads model gives you more control
- Money-back guarantee on first pack reduces risk
- Credit expiration (3 months) is annoying
- Best as supplementary channel

---

### TaskRabbit

**How It Works:**
- On-demand task platform owned by IKEA
- Focused on small, one-off jobs (furniture assembly, mounting, minor repairs)
- Taskers set their own rates and availability

**Cost Structure:**
- Platform takes a service fee (percentage of job cost)
- Taskers set their own hourly rates
- Low barrier to entry

**Lead Quality Rating: 4/10 for real contractors**
- Designed for handyman/small tasks, NOT full-service contracting
- Jobs are typically small ($50-$300 range)
- Customers expect quick, cheap help

**Volume Potential: MEDIUM for small tasks**
- Good volume of small jobs in urban areas
- Not meaningful volume for real contracting work

**Effort to Maintain: LOW**

**Worth It? NO for professional contractors**
- Only makes sense for handyman-type operators doing small tasks
- Not a serious platform for licensed contractors doing $1,000+ jobs
- IKEA assembly and furniture mounting dominate the platform

## RECOMMENDED STRATEGY FOR SMALL-MEDIUM HOME SERVICES COMPANY

### Tier 1 -- MUST DO (Primary Channels)
1. **Google Local Services Ads** -- Your #1 lead source. Budget $300-$1,200/week. Optimize with reviews and fast response times.
2. **Google Business Profile** (not covered above but essential companion to LSA) -- Free. Collect reviews aggressively.

### Tier 2 -- SHOULD DO (High-Value, Low-Risk)
3. **Nextdoor** -- Free Business Page + encourage customer recommendations. Add paid ads once organic traction builds.
4. **Facebook Business Page + Ads** -- Build organic presence, run hyper-local ads with before/after content and video.
5. **Yelp Free Listing** -- Claim it, optimize it, respond to reviews. Zero cost.

### Tier 3 -- WORTH TESTING (Supplementary)
6. **Home Depot Pro Referral** -- Free if you already shop there. No-brainer add-on.
7. **BuildZoom** -- Zero risk, pay only if hired. Great for larger projects.
8. **Porch** -- Test with small budget, track cost per booked job carefully.
9. **Bark** -- Low-risk credit system, try first pack with money-back guarantee.
10. **Houzz Free Profile** -- Upload your best project photos. Great for branding even if leads are low.

### Tier 4 -- AVOID or USE WITH EXTREME CAUTION
11. **Thumbtack** -- Only as supplement during slow periods. Watch spending carefully.
12. **Angi/HomeAdvisor** -- Avoid the annual contract. Math rarely works for most contractors.
13. **Houzz Paid Lead Gen** -- Too expensive for the quality/volume delivered.
14. **TaskRabbit** -- Wrong platform unless you do handyman-only work.
15. **Yelp Paid Ads** -- Only if you are in a major metro doing high-ticket work.

### The #1 Rule for 2026
The contractors winning in 2026 are diversifying across 3-5 channels AND investing in channels they OWN (website, SEO, email list, Google Business Profile). No single platform should account for more than 30-40% of your leads. Platform dependency is the biggest risk in lead generation.
