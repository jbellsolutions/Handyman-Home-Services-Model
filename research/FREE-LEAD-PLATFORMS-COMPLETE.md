## 2. CLASSIFIED AD SITES

### 2a. Craigslist
- **URL:** https://craigslist.org
- **Cost:** 100% FREE to post services
- **Post Services:** YES - Post under "Services Offered" in your local city
- **Browse Jobs:** YES - Check "Gigs" and "Services Wanted" sections
- **Automation Feasibility:** MODERATE RISK. Craigslist explicitly prohibits bots in their Terms of Use and has sued companies for automated posting. They use IP-based rate limiting, CAPTCHA challenges, phone verification for flagged accounts, and ghosting (posts appear to you but are invisible to others). HOWEVER, scraping job posts for leads is technically feasible using rotating proxies and undetected-chromedriver (Selenium patches). Posting automation is riskier and more likely to get accounts banned.
- **Lead Quality:** Medium - high volume but lots of tire-kickers
- **Best For:** General handyman work, moving help, cleaning, painting, yard work, furniture assembly
- **Pro Tips:** Post in multiple nearby cities. Renew posts every 48 hours. Include clear pricing. Use phone number rather than email for faster responses. Morning posts (6-8 AM) get the most visibility.

### 2b. Locanto
- **URL:** https://www.locanto.com
- **Cost:** FREE to post in most categories
- **Post Services:** YES
- **Browse Jobs:** YES
- **Automation Feasibility:** LOW RISK. Less aggressive anti-bot measures than Craigslist. Simple form submissions. No API but web scraping is feasible.
- **Lead Quality:** Low-Medium (smaller user base, 20M+ monthly visitors globally)
- **Best For:** General services, available in 60+ countries
- **Pro Tips:** International reach is a bonus if you serve tourist areas.

### 2c. ClassifiedAds.com
- **URL:** https://www.classifiedads.com
- **Cost:** FREE
- **Post Services:** YES
- **Browse Jobs:** YES
- **Automation Feasibility:** LOW RISK. Basic platform with minimal bot detection.
- **Lead Quality:** Low (smaller audience)
- **Best For:** Supplementary posting, backlink building

### 2d. Geebo
- **URL:** https://www.geebo.com
- **Cost:** FREE
- **Post Services:** YES
- **Browse Jobs:** LIMITED - every listing is human-moderated before going live
- **Automation Feasibility:** VERY LOW (human moderation slows everything down, but posting can be automated)
- **Lead Quality:** Low but safe (strong fraud prevention)
- **Best For:** Safe classified ads, trustworthy audience

### 2e. PennySaver
- **URL:** https://www.pennysaverusa.com
- **Cost:** FREE
- **Post Services:** YES
- **Browse Jobs:** YES
- **Automation Feasibility:** LOW RISK. Traditional classified format.
- **Lead Quality:** Low (older demographic, print-era audience)
- **Best For:** Local services, especially in areas with print PennySaver distribution (reaches 11.2M Americans weekly)

### 2f. Recycler
- **URL:** https://www.recycler.com
- **Cost:** FREE
- **Post Services:** YES
- **Browse Jobs:** YES
- **Automation Feasibility:** LOW RISK
- **Lead Quality:** Low
- **Best For:** Local services, similar to Craigslist

### 2g. Oodle
- **URL:** https://www.oodle.com
- **Cost:** FREE (aggregator - you cannot post directly on Oodle)
- **Post Services:** INDIRECT - Oodle aggregates from 80,000+ sources including eBay and local papers. Post on partner sites and your listing may appear on Oodle.
- **Browse Jobs:** YES
- **Automation Feasibility:** LOW for scraping aggregated listings
- **Lead Quality:** Low-Medium
- **Best For:** Extended reach through aggregation

## 3. MARKETPLACE APPS

### 3a. Facebook Marketplace
- **URL:** https://www.facebook.com/marketplace
- **Cost:** FREE to list services
- **Post Services:** YES - Facebook allows service listings and has integrated with platforms like Handy for home services booking directly through Marketplace
- **Browse Jobs:** YES - monitor local buy/sell groups and "services needed" posts
- **Automation Feasibility:** HIGH RISK. Facebook has aggressive bot detection: behavioral analysis, device fingerprinting, account age scoring, and rapid ban enforcement. Tools like Axiom.ai and other browser agents can automate some tasks but risk account suspension. Facebook Groups are easier to monitor manually.
- **Lead Quality:** High - people posting are actively seeking help
- **Best For:** All home services, especially urgent/same-day requests
- **Pro Tips:** Join 10-20 local buy/sell/trade groups and neighborhood groups. Post your services once per week in each. Respond FAST to "ISO handyman" posts. Before/after photos perform extremely well.

### 3b. OfferUp
- **URL:** https://offerup.com
- **Cost:** FREE (primarily a goods marketplace)
- **Post Services:** LIMITED - OfferUp merged with Letgo in 2020 and discontinued the Jobs/Services section. You can still create listings framed as services but it is not the intended use.
- **Browse Jobs:** NO (not designed for service requests)
- **Automation Feasibility:** MEDIUM
- **Lead Quality:** Low for services
- **Best For:** NOT recommended for handyman services; better for selling used tools/materials

## 4. NEIGHBORHOOD & COMMUNITY APPS

### 4a. Nextdoor
- **URL:** https://nextdoor.com
- **Cost:** FREE - create a Business Page for free
- **Post Services:** YES - "Neighbors for Hire" feature lets you list services
- **Browse Jobs:** YES - residents post recommendations requests and service needs
- **Automation Feasibility:** HIGH RISK. Nextdoor requires address verification. They monitor posting frequency. Third-party tools like Syndr.ai can monitor for relevant discussions and notify you in real-time when someone mentions your service type. This is the recommended approach rather than direct automation.
- **Lead Quality:** VERY HIGH - address-verified neighbors, high trust, strong intent
- **Best For:** All neighborhood services - handyman, lawn care, painting, plumbing, electrical
- **Pro Tips:** This is consistently ranked #1 by contractors on forums like ContractorTalk. Engage authentically - answer questions, give free advice, build reputation. Recommendations from neighbors carry massive weight. Free Business Page with services, photos, reviews.

### 4b. Facebook Local Groups
- **URL:** Search Facebook for "[Your City] Buy Sell Trade" or "[Your Neighborhood] Community"
- **Cost:** FREE
- **Post Services:** YES (follow group rules)
- **Browse Jobs:** YES - people regularly post asking for handyman recommendations
- **Automation Feasibility:** HIGH RISK (same as Facebook Marketplace)
- **Lead Quality:** Very High - community-based trust
- **Best For:** Hyper-local work, building word-of-mouth
- **Pro Tips:** Be the first to reply to "looking for a handyman" posts. Most groups allow weekly self-promotion posts.

## 5. SERVICE REQUEST PLATFORMS

### 5a. Thumbtack
- **URL:** https://www.thumbtack.com
- **Cost:** FREE to create a profile. PAY-PER-LEAD: $8-$60+ per lead depending on job type, location, and competition. Handyman leads typically $8-$25 range. No annual fee.
- **Post Services:** YES - detailed service profile with pricing
- **Browse Jobs:** YES - see customer requests in your area
- **Automation Feasibility:** VERY HIGH RISK. Thumbtack has sophisticated anti-bot detection including behavioral analysis, browser fingerprinting, and rate limiting. No public API. Scraping requires specialized tools (ScrapingBee offers a Thumbtack scraper API). Automated responses would likely get flagged.
- **Lead Quality:** High - homeowners with specific project needs and budget
- **Best For:** Small to medium handyman jobs, furniture assembly, TV mounting, painting
- **Pro Tips:** Set a tight budget cap ($50-100/week to start). Respond within minutes - first responder wins 40%+ of jobs. Many contractors report 10-30% conversion rate. Optimize your profile with 5-star reviews.

### 5b. Bark
- **URL:** https://www.bark.com
- **Cost:** FREE to create a Pro profile. Credits cost $1.65 each. Each lead costs 1-10+ credits depending on job type.
- **Post Services:** YES
- **Browse Jobs:** YES
- **Automation Feasibility:** MEDIUM RISK
- **Lead Quality:** Medium-High - customers submit specific project requests
- **Best For:** Professional services, home improvement, events

### 5c. ProMatcher
- **URL:** https://www.promatcher.com
- **Cost:** 100% FREE - does not charge for any leads from your profile
- **Post Services:** YES - create a detailed handyman profile
- **Browse Jobs:** YES - receive leads for free
- **Automation Feasibility:** LOW RISK. Simple platform, less commercial.
- **Lead Quality:** Medium - free leads for painting, TV mounting, tile work, plumbing repairs, drywall, gutter cleaning, fence repair, deck staining, furniture assembly, and more
- **Best For:** Supplementary free leads across many handyman categories
- **Pro Tips:** Specify exact counties, cities, and zip codes you service. This is one of the few truly free lead platforms.

### 5d. FreeContractorLeads.com
- **URL:** https://www.freecontractorsleads.com
- **Cost:** Free trial with $100 in free leads, then pay-per-lead
- **Post Services:** YES
- **Browse Jobs:** YES
- **Automation Feasibility:** LOW
- **Lead Quality:** Medium
- **Best For:** Testing paid leads before committing

### 5e. Sweeten
- **URL:** https://sweeten.com
- **Cost:** FREE for contractors (Sweeten is free for both homeowners and contractors)
- **Post Services:** YES (must be vetted/approved)
- **Browse Jobs:** YES - receive matched renovation projects
- **Automation Feasibility:** N/A (application-based matching)
- **Lead Quality:** VERY HIGH - vetted homeowners with real renovation budgets
- **Best For:** Renovation projects, general contracting, kitchen/bath remodels (minimum project sizes apply)

## 6. HOME SERVICES DIRECTORIES

### 6a. Angi (formerly Angie's List + HomeAdvisor)
- **URL:** https://www.angi.com
- **Cost:** FREE basic profile. Paid leads: $15-$120 per lead. HomeAdvisor (Angi Leads) has a $350/year annual fee + per-lead costs.
- **Post Services:** YES
- **Browse Jobs:** YES
- **Automation Feasibility:** HIGH RISK. Sophisticated platform with strong anti-bot measures.
- **Lead Quality:** Medium (leads are shared/non-exclusive, multiple contractors get the same lead)
- **Best For:** All home services
- **Pro Tips:** The FREE profile alone can generate organic leads through Angi search. Many contractors on ContractorTalk advise against paying for leads due to poor ROI and shared leads.

### 6b. Houzz / Houzz Pro
- **URL:** https://www.houzz.com / https://pro.houzz.com
- **Cost:** FREE profile. Houzz Pro software starts at paid tiers but profile is free.
- **Post Services:** YES - upload portfolio photos, get reviews, list services
- **Browse Jobs:** YES - homeowners contact you through the platform
- **Automation Feasibility:** LOW RISK
- **Lead Quality:** HIGH - 40M+ homeowners actively looking to remodel/redecorate. Verified pros get 35% more inquiries.
- **Best For:** Remodeling, kitchen/bath, design-heavy work, renovation
- **Pro Tips:** Reviews are the #1 factor. Upload high-quality wide-angle photos of completed work. Participate in Houzz Q&A forums to build authority.

### 6c. Porch
- **URL:** https://porch.com
- **Cost:** FREE basic profile. Pay-as-you-go leads: $5-$100 per shared lead. Vetted Pro program: $360/year.
- **Post Services:** YES
- **Browse Jobs:** YES
- **Automation Feasibility:** MEDIUM RISK
- **Lead Quality:** LOW-MEDIUM (many contractors report poor lead quality on BBB and ContractorTalk - disconnected numbers, uninterested consumers)
- **Best For:** Handyman services, appliance installation, general repairs
- **Pro Tips:** Start with free profile only. Test a few paid leads before committing to Vetted Pro.

### 6d. Yelp
- **URL:** https://www.yelp.com
- **Cost:** FREE to claim and manage business page. Yelp Ads are optional paid advertising.
- **Post Services:** YES - full business listing with photos, hours, services
- **Browse Jobs:** NO (review/directory platform, not a lead gen platform)
- **Automation Feasibility:** N/A (listing platform)
- **Lead Quality:** HIGH - customers who find you on Yelp have strong intent
- **Best For:** Reputation building, review collection, local visibility
- **Pro Tips:** Claim your free page at biz.yelp.com. Encourage every happy customer to leave a Yelp review. Do NOT pay for Yelp Ads until you have 20+ reviews.

### 6e. Modernize
- **URL:** https://www.modernize.com
- **Cost:** Free profile available, paid lead options
- **Post Services:** YES
- **Browse Jobs:** YES
- **Automation Feasibility:** MEDIUM
- **Lead Quality:** HIGH - generated over $1B in revenue for contractors in 2024
- **Best For:** Home improvement, windows, roofing, HVAC, solar

- **Best For:** ALL trades, ALL locations. This is the single most important free platform.
- **Pro Tips:** THIS IS NON-NEGOTIABLE. Every contractor must have an optimized GBP. Add multiple service categories (handyman + remodeler + painter, etc.). Post weekly updates with photos. Collect 5-star reviews aggressively. Respond to every review. Use keywords like "[City] handyman" in your business description. Contractors on forums consistently rate this as the #1 free lead source.

| Handy | App-based monitoring | Manual only |

**TIER 3 - HIGH RISK (Manual Strongly Recommended):**
| Platform | Why It Is Risky | Consequence of Detection |
|----------|----------------|------------------------|
| Facebook (all) | ML-based behavioral analysis, device fingerprinting, account age scoring | Account ban (permanent), IP ban, loss of business page |
| Nextdoor | Address verification, posting frequency monitoring | Account ban, loss of neighborhood trust |
| Thumbtack | Sophisticated anti-bot, no public API, behavioral analysis | Account ban, loss of reviews/reputation |
| Angi/HomeAdvisor | Enterprise-grade detection | Account ban, loss of $350 annual fee |
| TaskRabbit | Integrity scoring, response time monitoring | Account deactivation |
| Amazon Home Services | Amazon-grade fraud detection | Seller account ban |

### Recommended Automation Strategy

1. **AUTOMATE posting** on Tier 1 classified sites (Locanto, ClassifiedAds, PennySaver, Recycler)
2. **SEMI-AUTOMATE Craigslist** with extreme caution: use rotating residential proxies, randomized posting times, unique ad copy per post, and limit to 1-2 posts per city per 48 hours
3. **AUTOMATE monitoring/scraping** on Tier 2-3 platforms: scrape job listings and service requests from Craigslist Gigs, Facebook Groups (read-only), Thumbtack (via ScrapingBee), and government bid boards
4. **NEVER automate posting** on Facebook, Nextdoor, Thumbtack, or Angi - the risk of losing your reputation and reviews is too high
5. **USE third-party monitoring tools** like Syndr.ai for Nextdoor, Browse.ai for general web monitoring, and Apify for structured data extraction

## APPENDIX A: CONTRACTOR FORUM INSIGHTS

Key takeaways from ContractorTalk.com, Reddit r/handyman, and Handyman Startup forums:

1. **Google Business Profile is king.** Every experienced contractor says this is the #1 source of free leads. Optimize it obsessively.

2. **Nextdoor is the secret weapon.** Newer contractors are reporting excellent results. The trust factor of neighbor recommendations is unmatched.

3. **Avoid paying for Angi/HomeAdvisor leads initially.** Forum consensus: shared leads, high cost, low conversion. Use the free profile only until you have steady work.

4. **Thumbtack works IF you respond fast.** Contractors who respond within 2-3 minutes report 30%+ conversion rates. Those who wait hours waste money.

5. **Facebook Groups outperform Facebook Marketplace** for services. People ask for recommendations in community groups constantly.

6. **Referrals beat everything.** Once you have 10-20 happy customers, referrals become your #1 lead source. Every platform is a stepping stone to a referral-based business.

7. **Do NOT spread yourself too thin.** Master 3-5 platforms first before expanding to all 45. Quality responses on fewer platforms beat thin presence on many.
